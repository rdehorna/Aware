Having a mood disorder

Being diagnosed with a mood disorder such as major depression or bipolar disorder can seem life altering. However, what does this diagnosis actually mean and how does it impact your life?

One step towards understanding and management of a mood disorder can be as simple as mood tracking. As we are all unique people, a diagnosis often only begins to identify the tip of the iceberg and describe the possible reasons why you may be feeling the way you are. In reality, a person’s mood is complicated and consists of a mix of emotions, ideas, and thoughts. There is no black and white when it comes to how you are feeling, as there can be many shades of color in between. The point is: mood is ever-changing and transient.

More importantly, understanding your own mood and seeing the trends over time can offer you more options to take control of your own mood, and to help you identify what you need in order to feel better. This analysis can even act as a contingency plan to alert you, your loved ones, or your registered physician that you may need some help.This help may be changes in medications or in lifestyle. Either way, you will have another option to better take control of your mood.

How does it work?

Sign­up on Aware website or mobile app using your iPhone.
Depending on your tracking preferences, you can choose to receive daily notifications or every 2­ to ­3 days asking you about your mood, to which you can respond by writing a sentence describing your day.

If you do not respond, over the course of 4-5 days, it will result in a text being sent to a friend or family member asking about your mood and well-being. This friend or family member will have the option of simply texting back a response about you, and this response from them will be added into your mood tracking chart and analyzed. This way, if you are simply busy, then someone will log how you are doing for you, and if you are actually not doing well, then your loved ones will know to check up on you.

If you do not want to interact with friends or loved ones, you will also have the option of talking with and receiving texts from anonymous, but pre-screened healthcare personnel.
